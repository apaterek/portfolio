Google Arts & Culture Hyper History with Vsauce2

Image / video: to be added

Turning a platform launch into content people chose to watch.

Google Arts & Culture was opening the collections of 50+ natural history institutions across 16 countries: 150 interactive stories, 300,000 new photos and videos, 30+ virtual tours. The problem with an archive that size is that scale isn't a story.

Rather than tour the archive, each episode pulled a single object out of it (a giant sequoia, a Titanosaur, Lucy, the last passenger pigeon) and told the strange human story attached to it. The strategy was to borrow an audience already there for exactly this flavor of curiosity, and let the archive be the payoff rather than the pitch.
