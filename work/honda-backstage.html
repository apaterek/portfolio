Honda Backstage Uproxx × Honda Stage

Image / video: to be added

Giving a car brand a credible reason to be in music.

By 2018 Honda Stage was entering its fourth year, and Honda expanded it with Backstage: a behind-the-scenes series on emerging artists, hosted on UPROXX.com and Uproxx's Facebook, built alongside partners including iHeartMedia, Vevo and Snapchat. The strategic problem with brand-funded music content is permission: audiences can tell when a brand is buying proximity to culture rather than contributing to it.

The format answered that structurally. Each artist received multiple episodes (Origin, Journey and Breakout): returning to where the first song was made, following the artist's working life and studio process, then closing the loop as the dream arrived. Rather than borrowing an audience for a moment, the architecture committed to the artist over time, which made the sponsorship legible as support instead of extraction.

As creative producer, I held the editorial line with Uproxx, writing across the series and supervising cuts to keep the tone closer to music journalism than to brand film. I also pitched artists for future episodes and contributed to creative direction on graphics and animation.
